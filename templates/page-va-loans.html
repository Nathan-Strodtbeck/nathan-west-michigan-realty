Do You Qualify for a VA Loan?

VA loans are available to veterans, active-duty service members, and eligible surviving spouses. Most borrowers qualify with as little as 90 days of active service. Common qualifying categories include:

🎖️ Veterans with honorable discharge
⚔️ Active-duty service members
🛡️ National Guard & Reservists
🤍 Surviving spouses of veterans

Not sure if you qualify? I can help you pull your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) at no cost — it only takes a few minutes.

Common VA Loan Questions

Can I use my VA loan benefit more than once? +

Yes. Your VA loan benefit is reusable. As long as you've paid off a previous VA loan (or still have remaining entitlement), you can use it again — even to buy a second home after moving.

Is there a loan limit on VA loans? +

Since 2020, there are no VA loan limits for borrowers with full entitlement. You can borrow as much as a lender is willing to lend — with $0 down — as long as you qualify on income and credit.

What is the VA funding fee? +

The VA funding fee is a one-time cost (typically 1.25%–3.3% of the loan) that helps fund the VA program. It can be rolled into your loan. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 10% or more are exempt from this fee entirely.

What credit score do I need? +

The VA itself sets no minimum credit score, but most lenders require a 580–620. I can connect you with lenders who work with lower scores and help you understand your options honestly.

Can I use a VA loan for a condo or townhome? +

Yes, but the development must be on the VA's approved condo list. I'll verify this before you fall in love with a property — it's a step many agents overlook.
